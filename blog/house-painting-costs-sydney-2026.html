Complete Guide to House Painting Costs in Sydney

Updated for 2026 — real pricing data from hundreds of Sydney painting projects. Know exactly what to expect before getting quotes.

How much does it cost to paint a house in Sydney? Interior painting typically costs $20–$60 per square metre, and exterior painting ranges from $30–$80 per square metre. A standard 3-bedroom home interior costs $3,000–$8,000, while a full interior + exterior repaint ranges from $8,000–$20,000.

These are the real figures from 2026 Sydney projects — not vague estimates. Below, we break down every factor that affects your painting cost so you can budget accurately and avoid surprises.

Interior Painting Costs

Interior painting is the most common service homeowners request. Here's what you can expect to pay in Sydney:

Per Square Metre Pricing

  • Basic repaint (same colour, good condition): $20–$35/sqm
  • Standard repaint (colour change, minor prep): $35–$50/sqm
  • Premium (extensive prep, feature walls, high ceilings): $50–$60+/sqm

Per Room Estimates

  • Single bedroom: $400–$800
  • Living room: $600–$1,200
  • Kitchen: $500–$1,000
  • Bathroom: $300–$600
  • Hallway: $300–$700
  • Ceiling (per room): $200–$500

Full Home Interior Estimates

  • 2-bedroom apartment: $2,000–$5,000
  • 3-bedroom house: $3,000–$8,000
  • 4-bedroom house: $5,000–$12,000
  • 5+ bedroom house: $8,000–$18,000

Exterior Painting Costs

Exterior painting costs more than interior due to surface preparation, weather considerations, and access requirements.

Per Square Metre Pricing

  • Single-storey (easy access): $30–$50/sqm
  • Double-storey (scaffolding needed): $50–$70/sqm
  • Complex surface (render, masonry repair): $60–$80+/sqm

Full Exterior Estimates

  • Single-storey house: $5,000–$10,000
  • Double-storey house: $8,000–$15,000
  • Large/complex home: $12,000–$25,000
  • Fence painting (per metre): $15–$35
  • Deck staining: $20–$40/sqm

What Affects the Price?

Several factors will push your painting quote higher or lower:

1. Surface Condition

Walls in good condition need minimal prep — just a light sand and clean. But walls with extensive cracking, peeling, water damage, or old wallpaper need significantly more preparation time, which increases the cost.

2. Ceiling Height

Standard 2.4m ceilings are straightforward. Higher ceilings (3m+) require scaffolding or platforms and add 15–30% to the cost.

3. Paint Quality

Premium paints (Dulux Wash & Wear, Taubmans Endure) cost more upfront but last 10–15 years vs 3–5 years for budget paints. We always recommend premium — it's cheaper per year of service life.

4. Number of Colours

A single colour throughout is the most efficient. Multiple colours, feature walls, and accent trims add time for cutting-in, cleaning brushes, and masking.

5. Access & Height

Properties requiring scaffolding, rope access, or work at height add significant cost. A double-storey exterior with complex rooflines can add $2,000–$5,000 in scaffolding alone.

How to Get the Best Value

The cheapest quote is rarely the best value. Look for licensed, insured painters who use premium materials and include thorough preparation in their price.

Tips for Saving Money

  • Bundle interior + exterior: Doing both at once saves 10–15% vs separate jobs
  • Book in off-peak season: Winter/early spring often has better availability and pricing
  • Minimise colour changes: Fewer colours = less time = lower cost
  • Maintain your paint: Washing exterior walls annually extends paint life by 2–3 years
  • Ask about warranty: Professional painters who guarantee their work save you money long-term

What Should Be Included in a Quote?

A professional painting quote should always include:

  • Detailed surface preparation (filling, sanding, priming)
  • Number of coats specified (minimum 2 coats of topcoat)
  • Paint brand and product specified
  • Full protection (drop sheets, masking)
  • Cleanup included
  • Timeline with start and completion dates
  • Workmanship warranty
  • Licence number and ABN
